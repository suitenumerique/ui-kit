# StorageGauge

**StorageGauge** is a small family of components for showing how much of a
storage quota is consumed. They all share the same core idea — a `used / total`
value rendered as a horizontal bar whose color shifts as the quota fills — but
differ in how much surrounding chrome they provide, so you can pick the density
that fits the space you have.

The label-bearing variants use Cunningham's `useCunningham` for locale-aware
number formatting and translated labels, so they must live under a
`CunninghamProvider`. Styles ship in `@gouvfr-lasuite/ui-kit/style` — import it
once in your app.

### StorageGaugeBar

The bare track and fill — no label, no wrapper. Drop it wherever a plain bar is
enough, or compose your own UI around it. → [Read the docs](?path=/docs/components-storagegauge-bar--docs)

### StorageGaugeButton

A compact, clickable row: the bar plus a `used / total unit` label, rendered as a
`<button>` that forwards native button props (`onClick`, `disabled`, …). → [Read the docs](?path=/docs/components-storagegauge-button--docs)

### StorageGaugeInformation

The expanded, panel-style variant: a header with a title, the usage label, and an
optional "more info" action, laid out above a full-width bar. Supports `title`,
`titleChildren`, `label`, and `labelChildren` for customization. → [Read the docs](?path=/docs/components-storagegauge-information--docs)

## Choosing a variant

- Need just a progress bar, or building a bespoke layout? Reach for
  **`StorageGaugeBar`**.
- Want a one-line, clickable quota entry in a sidebar or footer? Use
  **`StorageGaugeButton`**.
- Have room to show the quota with a heading and a "more info" link (e.g. a
  settings page)? Use **`StorageGaugeInformation`**.
